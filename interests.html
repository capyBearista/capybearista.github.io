Interests & Hobbies

Beyond the Screen

Beyond the screen, my main interest is people. I'm fascinated by what makes them unique, what brings them together, and the spaces they create.

Photography

This started with photography. I'm drawn to taking portraits because the process is what matters most. It's a collaboration. You're not just taking a picture; you're having a conversation, learning about someone, and working to bring out the qualities that make them who they are.

Community Leadership

I try to apply that same idea of listening and collaborating on a larger scale in my community work. For me, things like student government are about building a stronger community and making sure every voice is heard. It's about understanding the well-being of the group and taking action to improve it.

Coffee Shop Discoveries

And on a smaller scale, this curiosity is what fuels my ongoing quest to find the best local coffee shop. I'm always exploring, looking for that perfect combination of great coffee and a great "vibe." Each stop is a small moment, but it's a chance to appreciate the unique character of a place.

The Common Thread

Whether it's through a camera lens, in community meetings, or over a good cup of coffee, I'm always looking for those moments of genuine connection. These experiences shape how I approach everything else - including how I think about technology and the problems it should solve.

These interests aren't separate from my technical work - they inform it. The empathy I develop through photography, the collaboration skills from community leadership, and the curiosity that drives coffee exploration all make me a better technologist and problem-solver.
